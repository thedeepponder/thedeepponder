Ep. 24: Artemis II - Are We Really Going Back to the Moon?

Jan 31, 2026 • 46 min

In this episode, we explore NASA’s Artemis II mission—the first planned crewed journey beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo—and ask what it signals for the future of human spaceflight. We examine how space exploration is presented to the public, touching on questions of trust, narrative, and perception through references to James Fox, Buzz Aldrin’s family accounts, and the Space Cadets experiment.

Reflecting on a Trip to Egypt (Part 2) | The Deep Ponder Podcast

Jan 27, 2026 • 2 hr 23 min

In Part 2 of Reflecting on a Trip to Egypt, we continue the conversation after John’s journey through Cairo and Luxor, joined again by co-host Niall and guest Andy, who has also visited Egypt. The discussion goes deeper into Egypt’s ancient sites, exploring alternative theories about the pyramids and how experiencing these places in person reshapes our understanding of their purpose. We reflect on key locations—including the Serapeum of Saqqara, the Bent and Step Pyramids, the Valley of the Kings, and more—sharing impressions of standing within spaces that have held meaning for thousands of years.

Jan 26, 2026 • 1 hr 38 min

In this episode of The Deep Ponder Podcast, John reflects on his recent trip to Egypt, sharing first-hand experiences from Cairo and Luxor - from the Pyramids of Giza to the temples of Luxor along the Nile.
